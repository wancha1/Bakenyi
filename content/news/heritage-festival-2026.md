The annual **Bakenyi Cultural Heritage Festival** concluded yesterday in a vibrant display of colors, drumming, and competitive watermanship. Hosted along the sandy banks of the central wetlands, the event attracted an estimated five thousand attendees, including visitors from neighboring cultural institutions.

The highlight of the festival was the **Grand Canoe Race**, where teams from the *BaiseMugosa* and *BaiseKabalila* clans raced across a three-kilometer course. 

### Key Highlights:
* **The Lukenye Poetry Slam**: Younger generations composed and performed spoken-word pieces entirely in Lukenye, touching on themes of displacement, migration, and the healing spirit of the water.
* **Weaving Demonstrations**: Master craftswomen held live workshops demonstrating the intricate weaving of reed mats and papyrus baskets using dyes sourced from local riverbed minerals.
* **Heritage Exhibition**: A popup museum displayed replica tools, historic fishing spears (*Amafumu*), and ancestral lineage maps tracing the migration patterns of the Bakenyi from ancient times.

The festival acts as a vital bridge between the historical repository of our ancestors and the forward-looking ambitions of our children.
