The floating islands of Lake Kyoga played host to an unprecedented gathering this week, as elders, youth leaders, and cultural preservationists from all **14 recognized Bakenyi clans** came together. 

The primary focus of this historic assembly was the promotion of **Lukenye**, the ancestral language of the Bakenyi, which has faced severe pressure from surrounding regional dialects. Under the shade of lakeside papyrus groves, clan heads shared oral histories and laid out plans for a digital dictionary project.

### Core Agreements Reached:
1. **Lukenye Language Classes**: Establishing weekend learning circles for youth in primary schools.
2. **Canoe Crafting Mentorship**: Passing down the mathematical and physical art of carving single-log canoes (*Ebyato*).
3. **Floating Island Protection**: Working with environmental agencies to preserve the ecological stability of the *Ebiswa* (floating marshes) which are critical to Bakenyi heritage and fishing routes.

*"Our identity is anchored in the waters of Kyoga,"* noted Elder Moses Musuusu during his opening remarks. *"If the waters change, we adapt, but if our language fades, we vanish as a distinct people. This gathering is our anchor."*
